Powder Session At Snow Summit

In between creating two new apps and finishing up my portfolio, I got a call from my best friend on the morning of January 14, 2019. When I picked up, he was talking so fast that I knew something was up. Normally, he's calm and collective, but on this morning, he was pretty dead set on going to Bear Mountain.

I said I needed to think about his invite, and that I'd call him back in two hours. I didn't even get two hours, he was so adamant to go that he called me about 45-minutes later, saying that he booked a hotel room, that we're leaving that day and boarding fresh powder in the morning. He said, "Don't worry, I got a room with really good Wi-Fi, so you can work all night."

How could I say no? We left at 2 pm and got to Bear by 5 pm. While driving up the mountain, we knew we were going to score. The road was completely covered in snow, it was snowing pretty much our whole drive up the mountain, and more people were driving down than driving up. Once we arrived, we grabbed some food, and I worked on projects for about 5-hours until I went to sleep. That's the cool thing about programming, as long as you have your computer charged, you can literally work from anywhere.

When we woke up in the morning we were a bit skeptical. The snow in the parking lot was not as deep as it was when we got there the day before, and we worried that it might have rained over-night rather than snow.

Boy, we were wrong, we got to the mountain and the snow was incredible. We started in the middle run first and were getting powder on every turn we had. We then went to the left side of the mountain and took a couple black diamond runs. It felt like I was surfing, every carve, every turn, I was blasting powder, and best of all it started snowing. After boarding taking about 10-runs on the mountain, we started looking for tree runs, and places we could jump off the normal trail. This is something unheard of at bear because they usually don't get enough snow.

Once we found our line, we'd go for it. I've probably snowboarded Snow Summit 40+ times, and not once have I got backcountry powder runs there. After about 20-runs we called it a day. Although we couldn't get a surf session in at the end of our day, a random strike mission turned into the best powder day at Snow Summit we ever had.
